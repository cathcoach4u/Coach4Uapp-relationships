Your Nervous System and Conflict

Understanding why conflict can feel out of control, and what it takes to come back to yourself.

🔥
Baby brain in charge
Activated / threat state

What is happening inside

  • The brain detects threat and triggers a survival response
  • Stress hormones flood the body within seconds
  • Rational thinking and problem-solving go offline
  • The only available responses are fight, flight, or freeze
  • This is the baby brain running the show

What it looks like in conflict

  • Saying things you would not say when calm
  • Feeling unable to hear your partner clearly
  • Repeating the same point louder rather than differently
  • Storming out, shutting down, or going completely silent
  • Behaviour that feels out of character
This is not intentional harm. It is a nervous system doing exactly what it was built to do.
The threshold
When emotional intensity crosses a certain point, the brain tips from adult thinking into survival mode. This happens in seconds. You cannot reason your way out of it while it is happening.
↓

Coming back to yourself

  • Name itI think I am activated right now.
  • Leave the roomPhysical distance reduces intensity.
  • Wash your handsCold water grounds the nervous system.
  • WalkMovement helps discharge the stress response.
  • Slow your breathBreathe out longer than you breathe in.
  • Agree a return timeI need 20 minutes, then I will come back.
🧡
Adult brain available
Regulated / connected state

What becomes possible

  • The reasoning, empathy, and language centres come back online
  • You can hear and process what your partner is saying
  • You can choose your response rather than react automatically
  • Perspective-taking and curiosity become available again
  • This is the adult brain in conversation

What it looks like in conversation

  • Staying present without becoming flooded
  • Noticing feelings without being driven by them
  • Choosing words with care and intention
  • Being able to hear your partner without defending
  • Genuine curiosity about their experience
Regulation is a skill. It gets easier with practice and with time.

Both partners are responsible for their own regulation. Escalation during conflict is not a character flaw and it is not intentional harm. When you understand this, blame softens and repair becomes possible. The conversation is always available again once nervous systems are settled.
