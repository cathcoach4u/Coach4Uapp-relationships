Relationships

Capacity and Connection

How the weight you are carrying affects the way you show up with each other.

Full capacity
🌿
Resourced
Rested, supported, and present. You have reserves. You can handle friction with patience and respond rather than react. Connection feels accessible.
High tolerance. Generous and curious with your partner.
Managing
⚡
Stretched
Keeping up but running leaner. Some life stressors are active. You manage most things well but minor irritations land harder than usual.
Moderate tolerance. May need more reassurance or space than usual.
Reduced capacity
💥
Overwhelmed
Multiple stressors active at once — work pressure, parenting load, financial strain, or health concerns. Your threshold for conflict drops noticeably.
Lower tolerance. Small things escalate faster. Rest and support are essential.
Low capacity
🪯
Depleted
Running on empty. Sleep, support, or recovery are lacking. Reactivity is high. Connection efforts feel effortful or impossible. Even small requests feel like too much.
Very low tolerance. Protect connection by naming your state, not acting it out.
No capacity
🛡️
Flooded
Crisis or acute overwhelm. The nervous system is in survival mode. Meaningful relationship conversations are not possible here. Safety and stability must come first.
No available tolerance. Pause all non-essential conflict conversations until regulated.

What refills capacity

Sleep and physical rest
Time alone to decompress
Reducing or delegating load
Being heard without advice
Movement and time outside
Connection without demands
Naming your state to your partner
Practical support, not just emotional
Reducing decision-making pressure
Shared parenting or household load

Why this matters in your relationship

When capacity drops, the threshold for conflict drops with it. This is not a character flaw or a sign that something is wrong with your relationship. It is the predictable result of carrying too much.

Naming your capacity to your partner — I am really stretched right now — is not a complaint. It is information that allows both of you to adjust your expectations and support each other more effectively.

Stability in the relationship is not just about communication skills. It is also about protecting each other’s capacity to show up.
